Blockchain technology is revolutionizing industries by providing a decentralized and secure way to store and transmit data. At its core, blockchain is a distributed ledger that records transactions across a network of computers. Each transaction, or "block," is linked to the previous one, forming a chain of blocks. This cryptographic linkage ensures that once recorded, data cannot be altered retroactively without the alteration of all subsequent blocks, which requires the consensus of the network majority. Blockchain's transparency, immutability, and decentralized nature make it particularly attractive for applications ranging from finance and supply chain management to voting systems and healthcare. As it continues to evolve, blockchain promises to reshape how businesses and societies operate, fostering trust, efficiency, and innovation in diverse fields worldwide.
