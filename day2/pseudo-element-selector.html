Bitcoin, introduced in a 2008 white paper by an anonymous person or group known as Satoshi Nakamoto, is a decentralized digital currency that operates without a central authority or intermediary. It utilizes a peer-to-peer network to facilitate transactions directly between users, verified by network nodes through cryptography. This revolutionary concept of a decentralized ledger called blockchain underpins Bitcoin, ensuring transparency and security. Bitcoin's scarcity is enforced by its algorithm, which limits the total supply to 21 million coins. Initially met with skepticism, Bitcoin has grown into a globally recognized asset and payment system, attracting attention for its potential to reshape finance, challenge traditional banking systems, and serve as a store of value and medium of exchange in the digital age. Its impact continues to evolve, influencing discussions on technology, economics, and the future of money worldwide.
